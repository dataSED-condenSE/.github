## 🌍 Project Overview

As large language models (LLMs) transform software engineering, their energy consumption has become a pressing sustainability challenge. These models, trained on vast datasets from platforms like GitHub, offer invaluable assistance but at a significant environmental cost. The sheer volume of data required results in substantial CO2 emissions, challenging the long-term sustainability of LLMs.

The **condenSE** project addresses this challenge by developing methods to reduce the data required for training code language models, aiming to decrease energy usage without compromising effectiveness. This innovative approach aligns with EU Green Deal goals and UN Sustainable Development Goals, representing a step towards a greener tech future.
